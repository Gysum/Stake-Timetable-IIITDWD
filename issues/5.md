## Room capacity violation (Violation of seating limits)

**Summary**
The scheduler does not enforce or validate classroom seating capacities. It succeeds in assigning large courses to rooms that cannot physically accommodate the registered number of students. No warnings are generated when the student count exceeds the `Seating Capacity` defined in the master room list.

**Example Case**
- **Course**: `Mathematical Foundations of Computing`
- **Registered Students**: 215
- **Assigned Room**: `C002`
- **Actual Capacity**: 116 (from `data/Rooms.xlsx`)
- **Result**: The course is scheduled in a room that is **99 seats short**, with no system alert.

**Impact**
- **Physical Infeasibility**: The timetable cannot be implemented in practice as students will not fit in the assigned classrooms.
- **Safety Hazards**: Overcrowding creates significant campus safety and logistical risks.
- **Silent Failures**: The scheduler reports a "successful" run while effectively producing an invalid operational plan.

**Suggested fixes**
1. **Load Capacity Data**: Ensure `data/Rooms.xlsx` is parsed during startup to capture the `Seating Capacity` for every valid room.
2. **Active Conflict Check**: Add a capacity verification step in the scheduling engine. If `Student Count > Room Capacity`, the assignment should be rejected or flagged as a high-priority conflict.
3. **Unallotted Logging**: Move courses that cannot fit into their requested rooms to the "Unallotted" sheet with a specific reason: `"Insufficient Capacity (Required: 215, Available: 116)"`.

**Steps to reproduce locally**
1. Verify the capacity of **C002** (116) in `data/Rooms.xlsx`.
2. Verify the student count for **Mathematical Foundations of Computing** (215) in the input divisional data.
3. Run `python main.py`.
4. Examine the output Excel; the course will be assigned to **C002** without any indicated error.
